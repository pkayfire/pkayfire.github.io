Graduating college is a strange feeling. I'm guessing it's the feeling you get at the intersection of freedom and responsibility. I've woken up the past couple days without any **looming** academic deadlines. I'm really not sure whether to be refreshed or scared that I'm entering the *real* world.

So what's **next**?

I want to be constantly **building**.

[Kevin] and I have spent the last week or so tinkering around on social media. I didn't notice (or frankly care about) it until Kevin showed me, but there's some interesting trends in how these accounts with > 1MM followers **grow**. We see lots of potential to hack around and have some fun :).

I was recently accepted to the next iteration of [PennApps]. I'm almost done assembling a team, and I can't wait to be building cool things with hackers from all around the world.

I'll be heading to [Stripe] as an engineer[^1] in February. I was cautious going into recruiting, really trying to find a place where I could grow as both an engineer and person. After one of the **best** interview experiences [^2] I've had, I can't wait to get started.

Looking back and seeing how far I've come since freshman year, when I couldn't write a single line of code, my one goal is to maintain **velocity**. By surrounding myself with amazing people, I'm optimistic. 

Here we go, *one step at a time*.
